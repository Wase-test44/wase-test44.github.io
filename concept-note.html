Concept Note

AREWA Progressive Eagle Movement

Highly esteemed and distinguished fellows,

You are aware that terrorism, violence, and insecurity, coupled with current environmental issues such as pollution, environmental degradation, resources depletion, ozone layer depletion, environmental health issues, and climate change including lower rainfall have led to competition and sometimes conflict over limited resources. Deforestation and overgrazing have further denuded our environment which the largely nomadic Fulanis rely on to survive and has led many of them to migrate to different areas, putting pressure on already stretched basic services, such as health and education.

AREWA has been battling with multi-dimensional security situations including the Boko Haram insurgency in the north-east, farmer-herders conflicts as well as ethno-religious conflicts in the north-central, and banditry in the north-west.

We are blessed with rich and abundant natural resources, yet we are living with hunger and in poverty. Our land is good for agriculture but we are being discouraged from going into farming. The seeds we are cultivating are not producing high and quality yield. We lack adequate value chains for our agricultural commodities. Banditry, insurgency and other crimes have affected both crops and animal farming that provide raw materials to our industries. These accumulated problems have caused staff layoffs, shutdown of industries and displaced investors; that is why our youths migrate to other areas for survival.

Industrial waste and heavy metal contamination in the environment has greatly affected human health and the ecosystem. It contributes to global warming, climate change, air, soil and water pollution, human diseases and species extinction. There is low performance of workers in our industries. Most of our youths are not willing to work with private organizations. Our corporate organizations are not much concerned about carrying out their corporate social responsibilities which is why many could not survive when there is a bear market.

Our population is speedily increasing, our economic resources are dwindling, hardship is everywhere, life is becoming unbearable and the government is facing myriad of problems including security and corruption due to failure in our responsibilities.
